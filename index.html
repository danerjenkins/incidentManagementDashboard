The following dashboards were built to surface operational insights from IT incident management data. Each view targets a specific dimension of service performance — from long-term trends to priority distribution — to support both day-to-day operations and strategic planning conversations.

▼   SCROLL TO BEGIN   ▼
01

Trend Analysis

Overview

This dashboard tracks incident volume over time, surfacing patterns in how tickets are created and resolved across reporting periods. Use it to identify seasonal spikes, evaluate the impact of process changes, and communicate workload trends to stakeholders at a glance.

02

Aging Incidents

Overview

This view highlights incidents that have remained open beyond expected resolution windows. By segmenting tickets by age bracket, teams can prioritize long-running issues, reduce SLA breaches, and hold assignees accountable before problems escalate.

03

Root Cause

Overview

The Root Cause dashboard categorizes incidents by their underlying source, enabling the team to shift from reactive firefighting to proactive prevention. Recurring root causes become visible quickly, guiding where permanent fixes and knowledge-base investments will yield the greatest reduction in ticket volume.

04

Incidents Created by Priority

Overview

This dashboard breaks down newly created incidents by priority level, giving a clear picture of the incoming severity distribution. Consistently high volumes of P1 or P2 tickets may signal systemic issues, while shifts in the priority mix over time can reveal the downstream effects of infrastructure changes or user-education initiatives.

05

Incidents Open by Priority

Overview

A real-time snapshot of the current open backlog segmented by priority. This view helps leadership assess whether high-priority work is being appropriately staffed and allows teams to course-correct resource allocation before critical incidents age into SLA violations.

06

Incident Count by Priority

Overview

This aggregate view presents the total volume of incidents across all priority tiers for a selected time period. It provides a concise, executive-level summary of service desk load and supports benchmarking against targets or historical baselines.

07

Interactions

Overview

The Interactions dashboard tracks every touchpoint between analysts and end users throughout the incident lifecycle — calls, chats, emails, and portal submissions. Understanding interaction volume and channel distribution informs staffing models and highlights opportunities to improve self-service deflection rates.
